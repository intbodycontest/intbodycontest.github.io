    Frequently Asked Questions - Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act

                                                                                      

    What is FATCA?show/hide

    FATCA, the Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act came into effect on 18 March 2010. FATCA requires that foreign financial institutions either enter into an agreement with the US authorities under which they must perform certain obligations, or incur 30% US withholding tax on all US-source payments and a certain proportion of all non-US-source payments that it receives from other financial institutions (this withholding will apply both to income payments and to payments of gross sales proceeds). The main obligations with the US authorities include implementing procedures: to identify clients who are, or are substantially owned by, US persons; to report annually to the US authorities certain details about those US persons and their accounts; and to withhold US tax and close those accounts where necessary.

    How / why has FATCA come about?show/hide

    The US has concerns that non-US financial institutions have been assisting US taxpayers in hiding their assets overseas and thereby avoiding US tax. Recent high profile cases have led to (or can expected to lead to) material fines for the financial institutions concerned and to criminal proceedings in the US against the officers involved in the activities.

    Will Standard Chartered be participating in FATCA?show/hide

    Standard Chartered is participating in FATCA and is registering subsidiaries of Standard Chartered that meet the definition of a "Foreign Financial Instituion" with the IRS. It will carry out the due diligence, reporting and withholding tax requirements of FATCA in accordance with any relevant Intergovernmental Agreements and local legislation.

    Will Standard Chartered be reporting client information to the IRS?show/hide

    If the client is a US Person (as defined by the FATCA regulations), incorporated in the US, or a US beneficial owner of certain categories of entities (again as defined in the FATCA regulations), then Standard Chartered is intending to report aspect of their financial activities to the IRS as required by the FATCA regulations.

    Will Standard Chartered be withholding tax on US source payments?show/hide

    Standard Chartered is intending to withhold tax on US source payments to FFIs that are not participating in FATCA (non-participating FFIs) and on payments to clients who do not provide the required documentation ("recalcitrant clients") in accordance with the FATCA regulations from 1 July 2014.

    If a client does not have any US connection will Standard Chartered still require additional documentation from clients for FATCA?show/hide

    For clients that are individuals there are occasions where we may require US tax forms even if the clients are not US. It may be that there is some information in the client documentation that indicates US status and therefore further due diligence is required.

    For clients that are entities, please note that we will require US tax forms from all clients. The form certifies whether or not clients have any US tax reporting responsibilities. If we cannot receive this tax information from clients, please note that we may be required to withhold tax on certain US source payments.

    Will changes to client documentation be required?show/hide

    On some occasions we may be required to make changes to client documentation to accommodate the reporting and withholding requirements of FATCA. Please note that if these changes are not agreed to, it may be necessary, on occasion to close accounts in order for us to comply with the legislation.

    Does FATCA apply to Financial Institutions that don't have Individual Clients?show/hide

    Yes. FATCA Regulations require Financial Institutions to report on aspects of the financial activities of "US Persons". "US Persons" includes a partnership or corporation that is incorporated in the United States. A Financial Institution that is participating in FATCA will also need to withhold tax on US source payments it makes to any FI counterparty that is not participating in FATCA.

    If a financial institution doesn't have US clients, will FATCA apply?show/hide

    Providing certain conditions are met, the Regulations state that it may be possible for non-US Financial Institutions without any US clients to attain "Deemed Compliant" status under FATCA. These FIs will still need to register with the IRS and put in place procedures to accurately identify a US client at account opening and to monitor for any existing client that changes its status to US.

    How does a financial institution know if FATCA applies to it?show/hide

    FATCA Regulations state that FATCA applies to all non-US "Financial Institutions". Clients should consult the FATCA Regulations to see if they meet the definition of "Financial Institution". The FATCA Regualtions can be downloaded from the IRS website.

    When does FATCA come into effect?show/hide

    The Regulations state that requirements of FATCA come into effect on 1 July 2014 with further requirements coming into effect on 1 January 2015.

    Where can more information on FATCA be found?show/hide

    More information on FATCA, including the regulations, can be found on the IRS's website. Clients could also contact their independent tax advisors.
