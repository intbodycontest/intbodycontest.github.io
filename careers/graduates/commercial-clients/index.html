      Commercial Clients

      Our programme is designed to set you up for success in your first full-time role. What you do and where you go across the breadth of our business is up to you.

      What is Commercial Clients?

      Commercial Clients provides solutions for companies during one of their most exciting phases; the critical growth transition from a small business to an entry level corporate. These can be entrepreneurs and local businesses on one end of the spectrum to mature or fast growing middle market companies at the other, most who have expanded internationally.

      Our team helps them facilitate trade with their buyers and suppliers, manage their working capital and transactional needs, assist them in managing volatility (currency, commodity prices, interest rates), as well as provide markets and corporate finance solutions when their needs become more complex.

      What would a year on our International Graduate Programme look like?

      We’ve designed a 12-month programme that will expose you to a variety of work so you can develop all the necessary skills to be successful at Standard Chartered. This is what you can expect during the year...

      • Induction - This is where you'll get to know how Standard Chartered operates in your location. We'll set you up to get started.
      • Month 1: Rotation in Operations - You’ll learn the fundamentals of how client transactions are processed and how we service our clients.
      • Month 2-3: Induction in Shanghai (5 Weeks) - Get ready for an incredible experience. This is your ultimate induction to the Bank. You’ll make connections from around the world and learn everything you need to be successful in your rotations.
      • Month 4-6: Rotation in Risk - You'll learn about our approach to risk and how we manage it effectively.
      • Month 7-9: Rotation in Front Office 1 - This is a critical rotation spending time learning key controls around client relationships.
      • Month 10-12: Rotation in Front Office 2 - Support end-to-end client activity in the role of an Assistant Relationship Manager.
      • Deployment - Transition to your first full-time role.

      More about the programme

      What sort of work will you be involved in?show/hide
      • Banking is an exciting and dynamic industry. In your first year you will be given the broadest possible exposure to our business. These are some of the projects and experiences you can expect:
      • Drive client loan/trade utilisation and expand existing client relationships through pro-active contact and strategic planning.
      • Partner with Relationship Managers on structuring client transactions.
      • Work directly with new clients on needs analysis and service requests.
      • Liaise with clients to manage the documentation and client due diligence (CDD) process.

      What are the application requirements?show/hide

      To apply for this stream you must have the following:

      • An undergraduate degree
      • The legal right to work in the country that you are applying to
      • Business level English (the ability to speak more than one language will be highly regarded).

      Advice from some of our Alumni show/hide

      Martin Huang - Manager, Process & Governance Commercial Clients - Taiwan

      “Internships and advice from others helped me to discover my strengths.” 

      I studied Industrial Engineering & Operations Research internationally, at Columbia University.
      After completing a few internships in different industries I found that I had a passion for Financial Services. I felt like my strengths in numbers and analytical thinking would be a great career to get in to.

      Before making a final decision I consulted some of the senior alumni and professors from my university. They helped me to see that with my strengths, I could be a great fit for the banking industry.

      Standard Chartered is present in the most vibrant markets in the world, Asia, Africa and the Middle East. The fact that I would be exposed to these emerging markets every day really excited me.

      Since joining the Commercial Clients stream I have been exposed to a whole range of projects, from comprehensive credit risk analysis to providing high quality customer service and solutions. There’s never a dull moment.

       Allae Souka - Relationship Manager Commercial Clients - UAE

      “I think it’s really important to get the perspective of others before making a big decision.”

      I studied Accounting, Financial Management and Mathematics at University, so my experience during studying was very banking related. I was attracted to the industry and the technical aspect of banking and how it drives the economy. I wanted to work in a top bank.

      Before I decided on a graduate programme I wanted to speak to as many people as I could from the industry. I think it’s really important to get the perspective of those who have moved around different banks, both local and international, as well as related industries. It helped to build a realistic preview of what to expect.

      Speaking to people who had been through the International Graduate Programme helped me to realise that learning in Standard Chartered is very strong and gives you credibility in the market.

      I chose the Commercial Clients stream because it fit in well with my strengths. Since joining I’ve been exposed to a number of different areas, and each area has helped me to build my network and my skills. I’ve worked on credit analysis and been involved in client meetings, dealing with trade finance and advising clients. It’s been a year of exciting and varied work.
