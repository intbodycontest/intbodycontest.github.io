      Corporate & Institutional Clients and Corporate Finance

      Our programme is designed to set you up for success in your first full-time role. What you do and where you go across the breadth of our business is up to you.

      What is Corporate & Institutional Clients and Corporate Finance?

      • Corporate & Institutional Clients (C&IC) - C&IC services the banking needs of global and large scale enterprises, financial institutions and governments. Getting to know our biggest clients, their businesses and challenges is the role of the C&IC team. They provide global expertise and local support, and are responsible for bringing together the right people to offer the right solutions for our clients.
      • Corporate Finance - Corporate Finance helps clients build their businesses through financial and strategic advice. The team offers a range of products including Mergers and Acquisitions, Leveraged Finance, Equity Corporate Finance, Project and Export Finance, Structured Trade Finance, Principal Finance and Structured Finance.

      What would a year on our International Graduate Programme look like?

      We’ve designed a 12-month programme that will expose you to a variety of work so you can develop all the necessary skills to be successful at Standard Chartered. This is what you can expect during the year...

      • Induction - This is where you'll get to know how Standard Chartered operates in your location. We'll set you up to get started.
      • Month 1: Rotation in Operations - You’ll learn the fundamentals of how we service our clients.
      • Month 2-3: Induction in Shanghai (5 Weeks) - Get ready for an incredible experience. This is your ultimate induction to the Bank. You’ll make connections from around the world and learn everything you need to be successful in your rotations.
      • Month 4-6: Rotation in Risk - You’ll learn about our approach to risk and how we manage it effectively.
      • Month 7-9: Front Office rotation in Corporate Finance - Join our Financing or Advisory teams and get hands on in deal initiation and execution, including pitching, research, valuation and financial modelling.
      • Month 10-12: Front Office Rotation in C&IC - Start building business relationships with clients. Be involved in account planning, pipeline management, credit risk initiation, approval and monitoring processes as well as learning the key systems used.
      • Deployment - Transition to your first full-time role.

      What sort of work will you be involved in?show/hide

      Banking is an exciting and dynamic industry. In your first year you will be given the broadest possible exposure to our business. These are some of the projects and experiences you can expect:

      • Get involved in building financial models and provide analysis and insight on the impact of different transactions.
      • Help prepare and pitch deals to clients.
      • Work on critical research projects, providing insights to our teams on key markets, industries and clients.
      • Work with teams across the Bank to manage approval processes and onboard new clients.

      What are the application requirements?show/hide

      To apply for this stream you must have the following:

      • An undergraduate degree
      • The legal right to work in the country that you are applying to
      • Business level English (the ability to speak more than one language will be highly regarded).

      Advice from some Graduate Alumnishow/hide

      Hasan AlShuwaikh - Analyst Mergers & Acquisitions - Singapore

      “Trying, failing and getting back up again made me realise what I’m good at.”

      I studied internationally, in San Diego. I loved the different cultural experience it gave me, constantly meeting new people and playing a part in something that felt so different to me. But it also made choosing what to do after University more stressful. I was torn between staying overseas and coming home.

      Before joining the International Graduate Programme I worked full time at one of the big accounting firms. Having this experience and through the cycle of trying, failing and getting back up again, I soon realised what I was good at.

      The decision I made to leave my job and start a career in banking was difficult. I was confused. I was taking a risk. Giving up something that was good in the hope of doing something better.

      I was fortunate enough to have several mentors in the industry. They helped me to see what a career in banking would lead to in the short, medium and long-term. I chose to join the Corporate & Institutional Clients and Corporate Finance stream because it provided me with an opportunity to make a difference through two of my greatest strengths. My analytical approach and building relationships.

      Tee Wei Jee (Jake) - Analyst - Singapore

      “Emerging markets are incredibly exciting for me.”

      When I was at University banking was always a career path that I was considering, but I wanted to know what my future could look like.

      I reached out to some of my friends who were in the industry, they shared their experiences with me. They told me what their day-to-day work was like and what excited them most about what they did. What I heard from them made me confident that banking was a good choice for me. And definitely something that had a bright future.

      When I was considering Standard Chartered, the emerging markets focus jumped out at me. Emerging markets are incredibly exciting for me. And I couldn’t find that exposure in any other bank. The toughest decision was choosing a stream. But I was prepared to back myself, I thought that being able to adapt quickly and work hard would make me a good fit.

      Your first day at work is daunting. It’s kind of like starting over again, with new people and a new environment. But my team was really open, and speaking to them helped me to see the importance of moving out of my comfort zone.
