    Linda Aredo

    International Graduate - Consumer Banking

    African Country Ambassador

    This is me

    Hi, my name is Linda. I was born and raised in Kenya, I went to University here too, studying Business Administration.

    I love Africa very much, but after having finished my first degree I decided to go to the UK to get my Masters in Marketing Management at the University of Kent. I wanted to get the best education possible and be exposed to a greater variety of people who thought differently about the world. What I didn’t realise was that deciding what to do after University was going to be so difficult…

    The end of University, the beginning of my career

    I was very adamant about wanting to stay in the UK after I had finished studying. I applied for lots of jobs, and had some very positive interviews. In many cases I had the right skills, and knowledge. The one thing I didn’t have was a permanent working visa. In the meantime I took a Sales Assistant role.

    Many people in my country think that the fact that you’re working in the UK means that you’re successful. The reality is that I felt I wasn’t reaching my full potential. That I wasn’t getting a return on the huge investment I’d made gaining my masters. I was frustrated, confused and unfulfilled.

    It was speaking to a friend of mine from back home that really opened my eyes. He asked me, “Why are you trying so hard to get a job here, and accepting less when you could go home where you know that your education and skills are in demand?” It was at that point that I realised that I would rather be making an impact and using my skills in Africa than staying in the UK, unfulfilled.

    Homeward bound

    As much as home was familiar to me is did take a while for me to adjust back to my culture. The UK is very orderly, and sometimes the way things work in Kenya just isn’t.

    I was expecting the job hunting process to be tricky; often it’s more about who you know. But applying for the International Graduate Programme with Standard Chartered was very straight forward and quick. It really surprised me.

    I’ve been with the programme now for over a year now. The exposure that I’ve gotten to senior leaders in the business has been amazing. Because most countries in Africa are considered emerging markets I’ve been able to work on exciting projects. This year I helped to launch a credit card product, something that is very new to the market.

    Experience like this isn’t available in the UK because so many areas are saturated. And I’ve discovered that it’s not just African’s who are drawn to working in Africa. I’m working in teams with people from all over the world, people who have adventurous spirits and the same ambitions that I do - to be part of something that has so much potential.

    One day I’d like to work overseas – and I know that with Standard Chartered that’s possible. But in the mean time I’m excited about being able to kickstart my career in a big way. And still have my friends and family close by. It’s kind of like the best of both worlds.

    Are you ready to take the next step?

    If you’re feeling just like I was after University and you’d like to ask me a few questions or get some guidance, I’m going to be hosting an online forum on Wednesday 13 November at 12pm - 1pm UK time. If you’d like to join then sign up here.

    If you’ve already made your mind up and you want a career at home with Standard Chartered, congratulations! You’ve taken the first step. Find out more about the programs, and how to apply. 

    Linda Aredo
