    Hasan AlShuwaikh

    International Graduate - Wholesale Banking

    This is me

    Hi, my name is Hasan. I was born and raised in Bahrain but when it came time for me to go to University, I decided to go overseas. I chose to study Finance at San Diego State University. It was a pretty big move for me. And I have to admit that the culture shock was huge.

    I was immediately out of my comfort zone. But I feel very strongly that some of the greatest opportunities lie in situations that are unfamiliar to you. And I was right. Because during my time in San Diego I gained international exposure, met incredible people, improved my English language skills and got a high quality education. What I didn’t realise was that deciding what to do after University was going to be so difficult…

    The end of University, the beginning of my career

    This was probably one of the most confusing times in my life. With very minimal mentorship available to me, I felt like I was on my own.

    The one thing that I was really focused on was making sure that whatever path I took it was going to be the right one in terms of building strong career foundations. And at first I really thought that this would be about finding a job in the US or Europe. But I couldn’t ignore the fact that I missed home, my family, my friends and all of the celebrations, especially around Ramadan. That’s when I started thinking about what a career back home might be like.

    Homeward bound

    It took a while for me to decide that I wanted to take my career home. But when I realised that I could still get the international exposure that I was used to and the comforts of home, I came to the conclusion that it was kind of like getting the best of both worlds.

    In the short time that I’ve been on the International Graduate Programme I’ve made so many friends from all around the world. During our training in Shanghai I met Senior Heads of business that have really shown me the opportunities that exist.

    I know that one day I’ll want to venture overseas again, and I know it’s possible with Standard Chartered. But right now I’m happy to be home and I’m proud of what I’m doing with my career.

    Are you ready to take the next step?

    If you’re feeling just like I was after University and you’d like to ask me a few questions or get some guidance, I’m going to be hosting an online forum on Saturday 30 November at 11am - 12pm UK time. If you’d like to join then sign up here.

    If you’ve already made your mind up and you want a career at home with Standard Chartered, congratulations! You’ve taken the first step. Find out more about the programs, and how to apply.
