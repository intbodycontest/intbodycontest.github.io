      Living with HIV

      Having correct information is key to understanding and preventing HIV, but there are many myths and misconceptions. 

      BookmarkPrint

      Do you know the facts about HIV?

      How much do you really know about HIV?

      Did you know you can't get HIV through kissing, swimming pools or toilet seats?

      The most common ways to get HIV are:

      • Through unprotected sex with someone who has HIV - this is the most common way of getting infected
      • Injecting drugs, using an unsterilised needle or syringe that have been used by someone with HIV 
      • As a baby of an HIV infected mother, during pregnancy, labour, or through breastfeeding. 

      Common myths

      Here are a few common misperceptions about HIV and AIDS:

      • HIV and AIDS are the same thing. HIV is the virus that causes AIDS. The virus damages the immune system, and someone is said to have AIDS when they develop HIV-related illnesses.
      • You can get HIV from touching, or sharing toilet seats with someone who has HIV. Sharing cups, glasses, food, cutlery, towels, toilet seats and kissing will not give you HIV.

      • HIV can be cured. There is no cure or vaccine for HIV or AIDS. But effective treatments now help many infected people live full, healthy lives.

      Some facts about HIV 

      • The only way you can be sure of your status is by taking an HIV test
      • Most people living with HIV don't know they are infected. The only way to know is to get tested
      • Unprotected sex is the most common way HIV is transmitted - it accounts for around 80% of all HIV infections. Don't take the risk; always use a condom.
