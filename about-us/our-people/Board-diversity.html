      Board diversity

      We have a distinctive footprint and a long history of diverse board membership.

      Currently, we have a diverse mix of ethnicity, gender and experience on the Board including four women and five different nationalities. There are twelve directors who have lived or worked across Asia, Africa and/or the Middle East.

      We believe strongly that a highly effective Board is about chemistry and behaviours, underpinned by robust processes. Our Board contains individuals who have diverse skills, knowledge and experiences that combine to provide different perspectives and effective board dynamics. In maximising the Board’s effectiveness, we take a long term, sustainable and measured approach.

      All Board appointments are based on meritocracy with the prime consideration being to maintain and enhance the Board’s overall effectiveness. Within this, we recognise that diversity in all its aspects, including gender diversity, is an important factor to take into consideration.

      As advised previously, the Board is pursuing a multi-year succession plan scheduled for completion by the end of 2016. As part of this, we aim that, by 2017, there will be 25 per cent female representation on the Board of Standard Chartered PLC. The Board Nomination Committee will oversee plans to achieve this, balancing all the factors needed to maximise the Board’s effectiveness.

      More broadly within the Bank, our employees represent more than 133 nationalities and 47 per cent are female. We believe that diversity, including gender diversity, is a very important factor impacting the Group’s performance and wider organisational effectiveness.

      Sir John Peace, Chairman
