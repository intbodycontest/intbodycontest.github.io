    What is FATCA?

    FATCA is US legislation aimed at preventing tax evasion by US persons and companies. It came into effect on 1 July 2014.

    Standard Chartered Group is supportive of the goal of FATCA and is registering its branches and subsidiaries that meet the definition of a “Foreign Financial Institution" for participation in the legislation.

    Standard Chartered Group’s subsidiaries and branches that have been registered for FATCA have received Global Intermediary Identification Numbers (GIINs) from the US tax authority, the Internal Revenue Service (IRS).

    Download our Global Intermediary Identification Numbers (GIINs)

    FATCA may require us to collect additional documentation from clients, in order to determine whether or not clients have any US tax reporting responsibilities. For those clients that have US tax reporting responsibilities, we are required to submit annual reports on aspects of the client’s financial activities to the US tax authority, the Internal Revenue Service (IRS).

    What we're going to do

    Where necessary, we will be contacting clients asking them to complete a US tax form.

    Amongst other things, it is important to highlight that we may be obliged to withhold 30% on certain US source payments (as defined in the FATCA regulations) from clients who do not return the completed tax forms, and “Foreign Financial Institutions” (FFI) who are not participating in FATCA.

    To ensure that we are not required unnecessarily to withhold 30% tax on any US source payments to you and/or report under FATCA, we require you to provide to us details of your U.S. tax status by providing one or more of the following forms.

    Forms                           

    Purpose

    Instructions       

     U.S person
    W-9 Which is provided by U.S. partnerships, U.S. trusts or U.S. Corporations (including certain branches of such entities outside the US). Instructions for form W-9

    Non-U.S person
    W-8BEN-E Which is provided by foreign entities (generally as beneficial owner). Instructions for form W-8BEN-E
    W-8IMY Which is provided by foreign entities acting in an intermediary capacity, foreign flow-through entities, or certain U.S. branches of foreign corporations. Instructions for form W-8IMY
    W-8EXP Which is provided by foreign exempt entities such as foreign governments, international organizations, foreign central banks of issue, foreign tax-exempt organizations, foreign private foundation; government of a U.S. possession. Instructions for form W-8EXP
    W-8ECI Which is provided by foreign persons that receive effectively connected income (e.g., as a result of conducting a US trade or business through a US branch). Instructions for form W-8ECI

    Non U.S persons (individuals)
    W–8BEN Which is provided by foreign individuals. Instructions for form W-8BEN

    Please fully and legibly complete and sign the form that is appropriate to your situation. Please return to your Relationship Manager or at Standard Chartered branches in the original or PDF format together with any supporting documents (as indicated on the form or instructions).

    If you do not know how to complete the form, or which form is appropriate to you, please visit the IRS website or consult an independent legal or tax advisor.

    Where relevant, we will be updating the legal documentation on the services and solutions offered, to enable the requirements of FATCA to be carried out.

    Please note that nothing in this page should be considered as tax advice and you are recommended to seek professional advice before providing us with a completed US withholding certificate. We are not able to accept responsibility if the wrong form is completed or if there are mistakes on the form. 

    Our compliance with FATCA in each country will be in accordance with any Intergovernmental Agreement (IGA) that a government has entered into with the United States for the purposes of FATCA and in accordance with local legislation.
